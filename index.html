Why AeSea

What We Facing

Hong Kong's ambitious energy goals aim to mitigate its high energy consumption, with a plan to reduce energy consumption by 40% by 2025 compared to 2005 levels. Recent data from Hong Kong's Energy End-Use Report for 2023 reveals that the commercial sector independently consumes 30,000 GWh of electricity. This usage equates to the energy output of one Little Boy Atomic Bomb every 5 hours.

Our Opportunity

Given the advancements in water cooling technology, Hong Kong may not appear to be an obvious candidate at first glance. Nevertheless, its coastal geography has facilitated the utilization of seawater resources. Currently, 85% of Hong Kong's residents employ seawater for toilet flushing purposes, amounting to the supply of seawater equivalent to 15 Olympic-sized swimming pools every hour.

What We can Do

Building upon this opportunity, AeSea presents a comprehensive system that harnesses seawater for flushing to cool air conditioning systems. By redirecting seawater towards condensers and integrating it back into the primary piping system, our solution offers the potential to diminish carbon intensity, enhance AC efficiency, and streamline maintenance processes effectively.

How It Works

Working Principle

The AeSea System leverages flushing water to extract heat from refrigerants, showcasing better efficiency due to water's higher heat transfer capabilities compared to air. In contrast to conventional centralized AC units that rely on ambient air for cooling, potentially exacerbating urban heat island effects, our system tackles this concern by dissipating excess heat into flushing water. This approach effectively contributes heat to Hong Kong's seawater flushing system, offering a sustainable solution to cooling challenges.

Integrated Dashboard

Within our solution, a dashboard is incorporated to enable remote monitoring and system control. This dashboard features temperature and pressure sensors integrated within the system, facilitating the implementation of a predictive maintenance system aimed at reducing unforeseen failures.
